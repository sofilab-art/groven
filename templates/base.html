How Groven Works

Structured Deliberation

Groven organises discussion as a tree, not a flat thread. Every contribution is either a Seed (a root argument) or a Branch (a response to an existing node).

Branch Types

Each branch carries a semantic type — proposed by the LLM, confirmed or overridden by you:

clarification extension reframing contradiction synthesis

If you override the LLM's proposal, the node is marked Contested — making disagreement visible.

Questions

Branches can also be flagged as questions — marked with a ? in the graph. Questions signal an unresolved thread that needs attention.

Synthesis & Voting

When enough threads diverge, anyone can click Suggest synthesis. The LLM proposes 1–3 concrete proposals that connect divergent arguments. You choose which to accept — they become nodes in the graph.

Every synthesis node is a votable proposal. Click one (purple halo) to read the proposal summary and cast your vote: Support or Oppose, each with a one-sentence justification. Anyone can vote, all positions are visible, and the vote arc gauge around each synthesis node shows the balance at a glance.

Votes shape the decision but do not make it automatically — they are advisory. The next two steps turn votes into a binding outcome.

From Discussion to Decision

open → ready → decided

Open — everyone contributes, discusses, requests syntheses, and votes on proposals. Two buttons appear: Suggest synthesis and Declare Ready.

Step 1 — Declare Ready. Someone clicks Declare Ready. This locks the discussion: no new branches, seeds, or syntheses. The LLM generates a structured summary of all positions. Voting on existing proposals stays open — this is the decision phase.

Step 2 — Record Decision. Someone clicks Record Decision. All synthesis proposals are presented ranked by vote balance, with the leading choice pre-selected. A different proposal can be chosen, but the decision-maker must justify the override. The decision is final and archived with a full vote snapshot.

The Graph

Click any node for details and voting. Node colour = branch type. Dashed amber borders = contested. Purple halos = synthesis (with vote arcs). Gold halos = decision.
